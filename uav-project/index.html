UAV Design Project

Autonomous 3D Printed UAV (Version 2.0!)

Current Project Status

7/19/26 - What's been going on?

Over the summer I have been prototyping the new version of the UAV, and making great progress:

  • The frame is fully printed and beats the initial 400g estimate by 50g.
  • The new propellers have been bench-tested and confirmed to deliver the expected thrust.
  • The final parts (wings and tail) are next up on the printer.

I'm hoping to fly by the end of the summer, ideally in Durham, with a second flight in Salt Lake City also possible. My 3D printer is on loan to the Duke Robotics Club for RoboSub 2026, so printing resumes as soon as I get it back.

Flight #1

Rest in pieces!

Unfortunately, the first flight did not go as planned. The EDFs did not have the thrust I expected, so the UAV could not accelerate quickly enough to take flight. Fortunately, every system independently worked, so version two will be ready soon!

Project Mission

Why a UAV?

This project aims to achieve several key objectives in UAV design and functionality as well as for myself:

  • Develop and refine my own aerodynamic design skills
  • Implement full autonomy for GPS waypoint navigation
  • Integrate a payload bay for sensor carrying or remote payload deployment

I love to push the boundaries of my skills, and this is a great project to do so. I first flew the UAV in the summer of 2025 and plan to fly version 2 during summer 2026.

Design Considerations

Key Factors Influencing the Design Process

Thrust-to-Weight Optimization

The newest iteration will have a ~1.4:1 thrust-to-weight ratio, as the new propellers provide nearly double the thrust of the previous iteration.

Aerodynamic Efficiency

The airframe design incorporates modern stealth aircraft principles while maintaining optimal aerodynamic performance. The large wings provide lots of lift while the teardrop shaped body keeps drag to a minimum. It also looks pretty cool.

V1 → V2 Improvements

  • Weight reduction through structural optimization (500g → 400g)
  • Enhanced propulsion system for improved thrust (1.3kg → 2.5kg)
  • Stronger wings to handle the increased thrust and prevent damage

Manufacturing Method

The UAV is designed to be entirely 3D printed, with an initial airframe weight goal of 500g — ultimately reaching 400g. This manufacturing approach allows for rapid prototyping and iterative design improvements while maintaining structural integrity.
